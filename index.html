About the conditions

Trauma

Trauma results from experiences that overwhelm our ability to cope. It can affect memory, emotions, bodily sensations, and relationships. Reactions can include hypervigilance, numbness, flashbacks, and avoidance.

Depression

Depression can make life feel heavy, slow, or hopeless. Symptoms include persistent low mood, loss of interest, changes in sleep or appetite, difficulty concentrating, and in some cases self-harm thoughts. It is a treatable medical condition.

Anxiety

Anxiety causes excessive worry, physical tension, and sometimes panic attacks. It can be managed with breathing, cognitive tools, and professional support.

Fear of death (Thanatophobia)

Fear of death is common and can be rooted in uncertainty, loss of control, or unresolved grief. Mindfulness, meaning-making, and therapy can help people live more fully despite existential worries.

Coping tools & self-help

Grounding (5-4-3-2-1) 
Look for 5 things you can see
Name 4 things you can touch
Name 3 things you can hear
Name 2 things you can smell
Name 1 thing you can taste
Box breathing (for calm) 
Inhale 4 seconds → Hold 4 seconds → Exhale 4 seconds → Hold 4 seconds. Repeat 4 times.
Quick panic tool 
Stop, slow your breathing, place your feet flat, name five things in the room, sip cold water, focus on exhaling longer than you inhale.
Journaling prompts (5 minutes) 
- What is one small thing I did today that helped me?
- What do I need most right now?
- Name a memory of feeling safe. Describe it in detail.
Fear of death exercise 
Write a letter to yourself from 10 years in the future. What would you want your present self to hear?
Audio-guided 1-minute breathing (optional)
Press "Begin 1-minute Breath" above. The animation and guided timing will run for one minute. If you feel faint or dizzy, stop immediately.

Immediate help & crisis resources

If you are in immediate danger or think you might harm yourself — call your local emergency number right now.

This site is not a substitute for professional help. If you are able, please consider reaching out to a mental health professional. If you are outside the US/UK, check local crisis resources — below are examples:

  • US: 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline)
  • UK: Samaritans — 116 123
  • International: Locate local hotlines at your local health service or use international directories.
